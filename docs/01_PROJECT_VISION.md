# Nidarsha

**AI-Assisted Policy Insights and Recommendation System**

> Making government policies discoverable, understandable, and actionable.

---

# Vision

Governments around the world invest billions into grants, subsidies, incentives, training programs, and support schemes intended to help citizens and businesses.

Yet a significant portion of these benefits remains underutilized—not because they lack value, but because the people who could benefit from them often never discover they exist.

Policies are scattered across numerous departments, written in complex administrative language, updated frequently, and rarely presented from the perspective of an individual or business trying to solve a real problem.

Nidarsha exists to bridge that gap.

Rather than asking users to search through government websites and policy documents, Nidarsha helps them understand:

* What policies are relevant to them
* Why those policies are relevant
* What benefits they may receive
* What requirements they must satisfy
* What actions they should take next

Our long-term goal is to transform government policies from static documents into practical decision-support tools.

---

# Mission

Build an open, explainable, AI-assisted platform that helps individuals and businesses discover and utilize government policies more effectively.

The platform should reduce the effort required to:

* discover opportunities,
* understand eligibility,
* compare available options,
* prepare applications, and
* make informed decisions.

---

# Initial Focus

The first release of Nidarsha focuses on **Micro, Small and Medium Enterprises (MSMEs) in India**.

The Indian MSME ecosystem contains hundreds of schemes across multiple ministries covering areas such as:

* Financial assistance
* Credit support
* Technology modernization
* Quality certification
* Export promotion
* Skill development
* Digital transformation
* Sustainability
* Innovation
* Manufacturing
* Entrepreneurship

Many businesses qualify for multiple schemes but remain unaware of them.

Nidarsha aims to improve that discovery process.

---

# Core Philosophy

Nidarsha is **not** designed to replace official government portals.

Instead, it acts as an intelligent guidance layer between users and official policy sources.

The platform should always encourage users to verify information using official government resources before making decisions.

---

# Guiding Principles

## User-first

Users should never need to know the name of a government scheme before finding it.

Instead of searching for a policy, users should be able to describe their goals, challenges, or business.

---

## Explainability

Every recommendation should clearly explain:

* why it was recommended,
* what evidence supports the recommendation,
* how confident the system is, and
* where the information originated.

Recommendations should never appear as unexplained AI outputs.

---

## Transparency

Whenever possible, every insight should link back to official policy documents, government notifications, or published guidelines.

Users should always be able to verify the underlying information.

---

## AI where it helps

Artificial Intelligence should simplify complex policy language, improve search, summarize documents, extract relevant information, and generate personalized recommendations.

However, deterministic logic should remain responsible for policy facts whenever possible.

Examples include:

* eligibility rules
* benefit calculations
* required documentation
* application timelines
* official references

---

## Open by Default

Nidarsha is intended to be an open-source project.

Our goal is to maximize public benefit while maintaining a sustainable platform.

Future premium capabilities may support hosting and continued development, but the core mission remains to improve public access to government policies.

---

# Product Vision

Nidarsha is more than a chatbot.

It is an AI-assisted policy intelligence platform built around several complementary experiences.

## Policy Discovery

Help users discover relevant policies by understanding their needs rather than requiring knowledge of policy names.

---

## Policy Insights

Provide structured, easy-to-understand policy pages containing:

* Overview
* Objectives
* Benefits
* Eligibility
* Required Documents
* Application Process
* Important Dates
* Official References
* Related Policies
* Frequently Asked Questions

---

## Personalized Recommendations

Recommend one or more relevant policies based on a user's goals, business profile, location, sector, and other contextual information.

Recommendations should include explanations rather than simple rankings.

---

## Guided Workflows

Assist users throughout the application journey.

Examples include:

* document analysis
* eligibility checks
* missing requirement detection
* application readiness
* next-step guidance

---

## Dashboards

Provide interactive dashboards for both users and policies.

Potential examples include:

* personalized policy recommendations
* saved policies
* application progress
* document management
* policy updates
* sector insights

---

# Long-Term Vision

Today, finding government support often depends on already knowing where to look.

We envision a future where anyone can simply describe their situation and receive trustworthy, explainable guidance supported by official policy information.

Over time, Nidarsha may expand beyond MSME policies into additional domains such as education, healthcare, agriculture, social welfare, environmental programs, research funding, and other public initiatives.

The long-term objective is not simply to answer questions about policies, but to help people make better decisions using them.

# Name

**Nidarsha** derives from the Sanskrit word "निदर्श" (Nidarśa), conveying ideas such as illustration, example, demonstration, or guidance.

The name reflects the project's purpose: helping people navigate complex policy information through clear, practical guidance rather than replacing official government sources.
